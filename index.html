About Me

Who's behind the schematic

I'm an Electronics & Communication Engineering undergraduate at National Engineering College, Kovilpatti, currently in my final year and maintaining a CGPA of 8.45. My interest in engineering isn't confined to theory — I like taking a circuit from a schematic on KiCad to a working prototype on a breadboard, and I'm just as comfortable debugging a sensor's noisy output as I am reading a datasheet cover to cover.

My core focus areas are Embedded Systems, VLSI, Digital Electronics and FPGA-based design — the layers where hardware and logic meet. Through my projects and internships, I've worked hands-on with microcontroller interfacing, sensor-based automation, and PCB layout design, building a practical foundation that complements my classroom learning in BJT circuits, combinational logic and PCB design fundamentals.

I'm currently preparing for campus placements with semiconductor and hardware-design companies, looking to apply this foundation on real production-grade problems — and to keep learning the next layer of the stack, whether that's an HDL, a new fab process, or a faster signal chain.
